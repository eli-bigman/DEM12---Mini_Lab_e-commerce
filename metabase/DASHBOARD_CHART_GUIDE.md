# E-Commerce Executive Dashboard: Narrative Guide

This guide explains the intent of every chart and how to interpret the dashboard in an executive storytelling flow (similar to your attached example).

## 1) Overall Business Health

Use these cards first for a 20-second snapshot:

### Total Revenue
- Source: analytics.v_kpi_summary
- What it means: Total completed-sales revenue.
- Why it matters: Primary top-line health indicator.

### Total Orders
- Source: analytics.v_kpi_summary
- What it means: Number of distinct completed orders.
- Why it matters: Demand volume independent of pricing.

### Average Order Value
- Source: analytics.v_kpi_summary
- What it means: Revenue per completed order.
- Why it matters: Tracks basket-size quality and pricing/upsell effects.

### Gross Profit
- Source: analytics.v_kpi_summary
- What it means: Revenue minus product cost.
- Why it matters: Better signal of financial quality than revenue alone.

### Customer Growth Rate (%)
- Source: analytics.v_kpi_summary
- What it means: Last 30-day signups versus previous 30-day signups.
- Why it matters: Early signal for future order growth.

## 2) Growth and Momentum

### Revenue Trend Over Time
- Source: analytics.v_revenue_trend_daily
- What it means: Daily revenue movement.
- Why it matters: Reveals trend strength, volatility, and seasonality.
- Reading tip: Look for multi-day pattern changes, not one-day spikes.

### Monthly Revenue vs Profit
- Source: analytics.v_revenue_trend_daily (monthly rollup)
- What it means: Revenue and profit plotted together over months.
- Why it matters: Shows whether top-line growth is converting into bottom-line growth.
- Reading tip: If revenue rises while profit is flat, margin pressure is increasing.

## 3) Mix and Composition (Deeper Dive)

### Revenue by Category
- Source: analytics.v_revenue_by_category
- What it means: Revenue contribution per category.
- Why it matters: Indicates which categories are carrying the business.

### Profit Margin by Category (%)
- Source: analytics.v_revenue_by_category
- What it means: Margin quality per category.
- Why it matters: Prevents over-investment in high-revenue but low-margin categories.

### Revenue by Acquisition Channel
- Source: analytics.v_revenue_by_channel
- What it means: Revenue generated by channel.
- Why it matters: Guides budget allocation across marketing channels.

### Orders by Payment Method
- Source: analytics.fact_orders
- What it means: Order volume and revenue by payment method.
- Why it matters: Improves payment ops and checkout optimization decisions.

## 4) Product and Customer Quality

### Top 10 Products by Profit
- Source: analytics.v_top_products_profit
- What it means: Most profitable products with units sold context.
- Why it matters: Helps prioritize merchandising, inventory, and promotion.

### Customer Retention Indicators
- Source: analytics.v_customer_retention
- What it means: Order distribution by customer segment.
- Why it matters: Shows where repeat behavior and customer quality are strongest.

## 5) Operational Risk and Post-Purchase Experience

### Order Status Distribution
- Source: analytics.v_order_status_distribution
- What it means: Mix of completed/cancelled/etc. orders.
- Why it matters: Tracks fulfillment reliability and process quality.

### Returns by Status
- Source: analytics.fact_returns
- What it means: Return case volumes and refund amounts by status.
- Why it matters: Quantifies return risk and customer experience friction.

### Monthly Active Customers
- Source: analytics.fact_orders
- What it means: Number of unique customers placing completed orders each month.
- Why it matters: Indicates whether the customer base is consistently active over time.

### Orders and Revenue by Weekday
- Source: analytics.fact_orders
- What it means: Completed order volume and revenue by day of week.
- Why it matters: Helps with staffing, campaign timing, and operational planning.

### Top 10 Countries by Revenue
- Source: analytics.fact_orders + analytics.dim_customers
- What it means: Geographic concentration of revenue and order volume.
- Why it matters: Supports localization, market expansion, and logistics prioritization.

### Monthly Refund Trend
- Source: analytics.fact_returns
- What it means: Total refund amount over monthly periods.
- Why it matters: Tracks return pressure and its impact on net revenue.

### Repeat Customer Rate (%)
- Source: analytics.fact_orders
- What it means: Percentage of customers with more than one completed order.
- Why it matters: Core indicator of retention strength and customer lifetime potential.

### Average Items per Order
- Source: analytics.fact_orders
- What it means: Average quantity of items per completed order.
- Why it matters: Helps evaluate basket depth and bundling effectiveness.

## Visual Design Notes (Color + Detail)

Card visuals are intentionally configured with stronger colors and value labels to improve scanability:
- Green-leaning tones: Profitability and positive performance signals.
- Blue-leaning tones: Revenue and volume trends.
- Warm tones (amber/red): Risk-focused views (returns and problematic statuses).

## Recommended Filtering Pattern

To mirror the style of the sample dashboard narrative:
- Start with overall KPI cards (business health).
- Narrow by time range and category/channel.
- Use mix and operational charts to explain why KPI changes happened.

## Suggested Executive Summary Template

Use this short narrative above the dashboard during reviews:

"Business KPIs are [strong/stable/under pressure] this period. Growth is driven by [top channels/categories], while margin quality is [improving/flat/declining]. Operationally, [order status + returns insight] is the main risk area to monitor next."
